# Motivation

Nowadays software is offered as a service, typically rented. Solutions are bloated, proprietary, and targeted towards enterprise customers that can afford to pay the price.  
As <a href="https://37signals.com/10" target="_blank">10</a> from 37signals states, that model serves well the Fortune 500 but leaves behind the Fortune 5,000,000.

We know the cycle.
1. Small and medium-sized businesses are underserved.
2. A startup breaks into the market by solving a problem for the underserved.
3. As growth happens, we observe a pivot to enterprise level deals.
4. Prices go up to support operations and shake out smaller customers.

You are at the mercy of the provider. 
* What if they go bust? You start from scratch.
* Increased pricing? Pay up or start from scratch.
* Feature request? It will be discussed internally.
* What about the code? It's a black box.
* Privacy? Five independent vendors operate in your environment, what privacy?

This has to change.
